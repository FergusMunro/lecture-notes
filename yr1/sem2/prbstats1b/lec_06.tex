\subsection{Assesment of Goodness of Fit}

\begin{definition}
    Suppose we observe i.i.d. RVs $X_1,...,X_n$. Denote the ordered values of the observed data $x_1,...,x_n$ by
    \[x_{(1)} \leq ... \leq x{(n)}.\]
  Let $F_X$ be the CDF of a distribution proposed for the RVs $X_i$. Then, a {\bf Quantile-Quantile plot} is a graph of
  \[x_{(i)} \text{ against } F_X^{-1}\left( \frac{i}{n + 1}\right).\]
\end{definition}
$ $ \\
If the RVs $X_1,...,X_n$ do indeed come from the distribution with CDF $F_X(x)$, points in the Q-Q plot should lie close to a straight line through the origin with gradient $1$.
